MIT is deploying over 500 AI-enabled surveillance cameras across its campus at a cost of more than $3 million, with capabilities including real-time facial recognition, crowd detection, and demographic classification by gender, age, and clothing colour. Data is retained for up to 30 days by default. The scale and capability of this system raises significant privacy, data governance, and civil liberties concerns in an academic environment.


> **Security Architect's Take:** Cloud security architects working in higher education or enterprise environments should use this as a prompt to review their own AI surveillance procurement policies — specifically ensuring data minimisation principles, retention controls, and access governance are embedded at the point of contract, not retrofitted after deployment.
