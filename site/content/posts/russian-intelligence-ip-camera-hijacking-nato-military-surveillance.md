Russian intelligence services are systematically compromising internet-connected IP security cameras across NATO countries and Ukraine to monitor military logistics, including weapons convoys and troop movements. The campaign was publicly disclosed in a joint advisory by Dutch civilian and military intelligence agencies (AIVD and MIVD) on 10 July. The operation represents a low-cost, high-value intelligence collection method exploiting poorly secured consumer and commercial IoT devices.


> **Security Architect's Take:** Audit any internet-exposed cameras or IoT devices within your organisation's network perimeter immediately — ensure firmware is current, default credentials are changed, and remote access is restricted via VPN or zero-trust network access rather than direct internet exposure. If your organisation operates near defence or critical infrastructure logistics, treat IP cameras as high-risk endpoints and consider network segmentation to isolate them from sensitive internal systems.
